Frequently Asked Questions

What types of files can I upload?

You can upload PDF, JPG, PNG, CSV, and DICOM (.dcm) files including lab reports, X-rays, and diagnostic summaries.

Is my medical data secure?

Yes. Uploaded files are processed securely and not shared. Files are automatically deleted after analysis.

Is this tool a substitute for a medical diagnosis?

No. This tool is for informational purposes only. Always consult a licensed healthcare provider for medical decisions.

What happens after I upload my file?

The file is analyzed, and key medical data is extracted and presented in an easy-to-understand format.

Can I upload more than one file at a time?

Currently, only one file can be uploaded at a time. Support for multiple uploads will be added soon.

What is DICOM and why is it supported?

DICOM (.dcm) is the standard for medical imaging files such as MRIs and CT scans. Our tool supports it for accurate image analysis.

How accurate is the analysis?

Our models are based on medical datasets but are not perfect. Always verify results with a healthcare professional.

Is there a file size limit?

Yes, files should be under 10MB to ensure fast and reliable processing.

Do you store my files?

Files are temporarily stored for processing and are automatically deleted. No personal data is retained.

Who can use this tool?

This tool is designed for educational and research purposes. It is not intended for clinical use.

Disclaimer: This tool does not provide medical advice. It is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis or treatment.
